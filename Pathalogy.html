Wheat

Loose smut

It is a seed borne disease; infection occurs during Loose Smut flowering through wind-borne spores,The infection remains dormant inside the otherwise healthy looking seed but the plants grown from such seeds bear infected inflorescence
Infection is favored by cool, humid conditions during flowering period of the host plant.

Corn

Common rust

Common rust is found everywhere in corn production. Both seed corn and sweet corn are very vulnerable to common rust, although the disease is not usually an economic problem in field corn. It tends to become less aggressive as the weather turns hot and dry.
Common corn rust, caused by Puccinia sorghi, appears as small, oval dark-reddish-brown pustules scattered over both surfaces of corn leaves.

Grapes

Powdery Mildew

Powdery mildew, caused by the fungus Uncinulanecator, can infect all green tissues of the grapevine. Tissues are generally susceptible to infection throughout the growing season.
High humidity and moist weather favours the development of disease.

Jowar

Nematodes

The leaves of plants affected by this start turning yellow. Along with this, knots are formed in the root and the growth of plants stops. As the disease progresses, the plants dry up
To avoid this disease, do deep plowing in summer season. Treat seed with 120 gm Carbosulfan 25% per kg.

SugarCane

Smut

Initial thin canes with elongated internodes later become reduced in length. Profuse sprouting of lateral buds with narrow, erect leaves especially in ratoon crop
Initial thin canes with elongated internodes later become reduced in length. Profuse sprouting of lateral buds with narrow, erect leaves especially in ratoon crop

WaterMelon

Anthracnose

Foliage spots are irregular and turn dark brown or black. Stem lesions can girdle the stem and cause vines to wilt
24-30°C and high relative humidity up to 100% for 24 h is optimum for fungal growth and infection with a thin film of water on leaf.

Cabbage

Fusarium Yellows

The disease is caused by the fungus, Fusarium oxysporum forma specialis conglutinans, and most members of the cabbage family are susceptible
The fungus usually enters the plant through young rootlets or wounds in older roots at transplanting time and then moves up the stem and throughout the plant.

Cauliflower

Downy mildew

Older lesions become dry, brown and papery in texture and may cause the entire leaf to drop
Cool, moist conditions favour the disease and the head of broccoli and cauliﬂower curds may be infected with black spots within or on the surface

Maize

Charcoal rot

Charcoal rot, also known as dry-weather wilt is caused by the fungus Macrophomina phaseolina, As the disease advances, leaflets yellow, then wilt and turn brown.The brown leaves remain attached to the petioles (leaf stems).
Charcoal rot thrives in the hottest, driest part of the growing season,when there is a high level of the pathogen in the soil and when plants are under stress from hot dry weather.
